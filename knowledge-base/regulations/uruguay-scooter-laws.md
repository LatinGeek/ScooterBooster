# ScooterBooster — Uruguay Scooter Regulations

## Legal Framework

### National Regulations

#### Ley N° 19.061 — Tránsito y Seguridad Vial
Uruguay's main traffic law. Electric scooters are classified under "vehículos de movilidad personal" (personal mobility vehicles).

**Key Points:**
- Electric scooters are generally treated as non-motorized vehicles when under 25 km/h
- Riders must be at least 16 years old
- Helmets are recommended but not strictly mandatory in all jurisdictions for e-scooters
- Scooters must have front and rear lights, reflectors, and a bell/horn
- Insurance is not mandatory for personal-use e-scooters (as of current regulations)

#### Decreto N° 118/021 — Vehículos de Movilidad Personal
This decree specifically addresses personal mobility vehicles:

- **Speed limit on public roads:** Maximum 25 km/h
- **Where they can ride:**
  - Bike lanes (ciclovías) — preferred
  - Roads with speed limits ≤ 45 km/h — allowed
  - Sidewalks — NOT allowed
  - Pedestrian areas — NOT allowed (must dismount)
- **Technical requirements:**
  - Front white light
  - Rear red light
  - Reflective elements
  - Braking system
- **Prohibited:**
  - Carrying passengers (unless vehicle designed for two)
  - Using headphones while riding
  - Riding under the influence of alcohol/drugs

### Municipal Regulations (Montevideo)

#### Intendencia de Montevideo — Ordenanza de Tránsito
Montevideo has additional local rules:
- Scooters must use bike lanes where available
- Maximum speed in pedestrian-heavy areas: 15 km/h
- Parking: designated scooter/bike parking areas only
- Fines for riding on sidewalks: 1-3 UR (Unidades Reajustables)

### Implications for ScooterBooster

#### Speed Limit Removal Service
- **Legal reality:** Modifying a scooter to exceed 25 km/h makes it non-compliant for public road use
- **Our position:** Speed limit removal is offered ONLY for use on private property and closed circuits
- **Disclaimer:** Mandatory acceptance before booking (see `speed-limit-disclaimer.md`)
- **We are NOT responsible** for how users use their modified scooters

#### Firmware Modifications
- Firmware updates that don't change speed limits are generally unregulated
- Custom firmware that adds features (cruise control, better battery management) is legal
- Firmware that removes safety features could create liability — technicians should document all changes

#### Technician Liability
- Technicians perform modifications at the user's explicit request
- The disclaimer protects both ScooterBooster and the technician
- Technicians should maintain records of all modifications performed
- Recommended: technicians carry professional liability insurance (not mandatory on platform)
